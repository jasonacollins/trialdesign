This subject is delivered over six weeks in a blended format, so you will have a combination of self-study time via the modules here on Canvas and online webinars and on-campus sessions at the UTS city campus.

In this subject, we study the principles and methods of experimental research. You will learn the theory and good practices to design experiments in the field and test hypotheses. We will review confounds that can appear in an experimental study and how to ensure that an experimental project delivers the right insights. We will also examine the generalisability of experiments and how we should review the existing experimental literature.

A useful resource to go into more depth is the book by Glennerster and Takavarasha (2013) Running Randomized Evaluations. A practical guide. You can download chapters online from the UTS library.

The unit is organised into three main modules and they aim to answer the following questions:

  • Module 1 - Experimental foundations What is a trial? Why do we run experiments? What are the different types of experiments?
  • Module 2 - Before the trial. How might you get buy-in for your trial? How do you define and diagnose a behavioural problem? How do you design or select an intervention?
  • Module 3 - Running a trial. What are the steps in a trial? How do we design our data collection and analysis plans? How do we choose an intervention? What are the threats to the integrity of the trial?
  • Module 4 - Statistical power and analysis. How do we develop and test hypotheses? What is the power of a trial?
  • Module 5 - Interpreting the literature. How should I interpret the experimental literature? Do experiments generalise?

Assessment

To complete this subject, you're expected to complete two assessments. The assessments are signposted with two different icons. The first, an open-book multiple choice quiz on course content in Modules 1 and 2, will comprise 35% of your final grade. The second assessment will comprise the remaining 65%. Your final mark will be calculated based on your performance on both assessments.

Click on the Assignment tab in the left-hand column to learn more about your assessments. There, you will see the assignments when they are published. Enter the individual assignments to read all the necessary information you will need to complete them.
